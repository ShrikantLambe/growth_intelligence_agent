# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Company Overview
We are a B2B SaaS company focused on revenue intelligence and analytics for mid-market and enterprise businesses.

## Primary ICP: Mid-Market SaaS & Tech Companies

### Firmographic Criteria
- **Company size**: 200–2,000 employees
- **Revenue**: $10M–$500M ARR
- **Industry verticals**: FinTech, HealthTech, EdTech, RetailTech, HRTech
- **Geography**: North America (primary), EMEA (secondary), APAC (emerging)
- **Funding stage**: Series B through Series D, or bootstrapped with >$20M revenue

### Technographic Signals
- Uses a CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, or Dynamics)
- Has a dedicated Revenue Operations or Sales Operations function
- Uses at least one BI tool (Tableau, Looker, Metabase)
- Has a data warehouse (Snowflake, BigQuery, or Redshift)

### Behavioral Signals (Strong Fit)
- Active pipeline tracking in CRM
- Monthly or weekly revenue reviews
- Dedicated growth or GTM analytics team
- History of buying analytics or revenue tools

## Secondary ICP: Enterprise (1,000–10,000 employees)
- Longer sales cycles (90–180 days)
- Multiple stakeholders: RevOps, CFO, VP Sales
- Requires SSO, SOC2 compliance, dedicated CSM
- Higher ACV ($50K–$250K)

## Negative ICP (Do NOT pursue)
- Companies under 50 employees (no dedicated RevOps)
- Agencies and consulting firms
- Companies with no CRM data
- Businesses with <$1M ARR

## Buyer Personas

| Persona | Title | Pain Points | Messaging |
|---------|-------|------------|-----------|
| Champion | VP Revenue Operations | Manual reporting, no single source of truth | "Automate your weekly revenue review" |
| Economic Buyer | CFO / Chief Revenue Officer | Growth unpredictability, forecasting accuracy | "Reduce revenue variance by 30%" |
| Technical Buyer | Head of Data / Analytics | Integration complexity, data pipeline maintenance | "Plug into your existing stack in hours" |

## Key Qualification Questions (MEDDIC)
1. What does your current revenue reporting process look like?
2. What happens if growth targets are missed this quarter?
3. Who owns the revenue forecasting process?
4. What tools are you currently using for growth analytics?
5. What is your timeline for implementing a solution?
